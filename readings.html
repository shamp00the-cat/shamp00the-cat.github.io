Reading Reflections

Reflection #1: In Plato's Cave, from On Photography by Susan Sontag

I really liked how Sontag said "To collect photographs is to collect the world"....."Photographed images do not seem to be statements about the world so much as pieces of it, miniatures of reality that anyone can make or acquire." It is eye opening also, to consider that all of what we know, visually, of the past is known through photographs, as all else is truly merely an interpretation. That being said, you can still take a misleading photograph. It's very true that "photography is not practiced by most people as an art". I think this only becomes more true as time goes on, with smart phones and the increasing popularity of social media. I agree that "needing to have reality confirmed and experience enhanced through photographs is an aesthetic consumerism to which everyone is now addicted." Yet, I still find them necessary to modern society.

Prior to this class, I used photography and image making in a variety of ways. There were times that I used it as an artistic medium, with my 35mm camera, and put a lot of thought behind the composition, colors, etc. That being said, I have definitley more often over the years used photographs and images unseriously over social media applications, mostly in joking manners, and also to record fun times. So far in this class, though, I have returned to a more thoughtful mindset when it comes to image making and photography. I'm happy about that and excited to see what comes of it.

I think in a way there is no way to take a photo that doesn't visually represent in some way the notions of our world at this moment. Of course, there is a way to thoughtfully do so and a way to thoughtlessly do so. I think memes and most social media content in general are examples of how to thoughtlessly do that. Ways to more thoughtfully do so would be documentary making, reporting, interviews, and photography of meaningful moments. I have always loved portraits as a means of this, so personally I would choose to represent visually the notions of our world at this moment by taking portraits of people, families, and groups, as well as video interviews and documentary style videos.

Reflection #2: My Stolen Future - Heela, from Motherhood in Crisis - Al Jazeera

I chose to read and reflect on Motherhood in Crisis.

When I first started reading about Heela and what happens to so many schoolgirls in Sierra Leone my heart sank. It’s so terrible to think about a girl being in school with her whole life ahead of her, just to have all her hopes and dreams being crushed by such a traumatizing event as sexual assault. The trauma of the assault alone is hard enough for a developing young person, but then the barrier to access of healthcare adding the burden of carrying out the pregnancy is a heartbreaking tragedy. A halt in their progression towards their goals. You could of course say that now their life is just different: as a mother rather than a schoolgirl. Of course, they’ll come to love the child, who isn’t at fault for what happened. But that doesn’t change the fact that it should have been their choice to make.

As the story progressed, I was relieved when I read that Heela was given the opportunity to be taken care of at the Rainbo Centre. I think it’s great that the facility is there to bring girls who are facing the same hardships together, and make sure they are cared for. The story was an emotional rollercoaster, from the horrible things that took place to the moments of relief, but more than anything I’m left wishing that Heela and the other girls who face this didn’t have to go through what they did. This story leaves you feeling conflicted, because it’s heartwarming to know that the Rainbo Centre is doing the immensely important work of helping these girls. Yet, you are still left heartbroken that the issue stands in the first place. I wish more than anything that young girls didn't have to face the things they do around the world, and that they weren't the ones left with the burden when the father abandons them. My heart goes out to Heela and every other person who ever goes through such a horrible incident as this.
